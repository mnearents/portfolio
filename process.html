My Process

Discover

I believe great products solve real problems. And real problems are discovered through intimate knowledge of the people experiencing them. This knowledge can be gained in different ways depending on the timeline and demands of the project.

To understand mental models, I love to interview people. Interviews can uncover all kinds of hidden gems of knowledge. If I want to create something innovative for pool owners, for example, I talk to them and find out about their pain points, their goals, and their workarounds.

Other methods of research include contextual inquiry (great for observing real behavior), qualitative and quantitative surveys, competitive analysis, analytics, focus groups, and subject-matter experts. The tools I use depend on time, budget, and resources available.

Design & Test

Once a problem has been identified, I run ideation sessions with the team to foster divergent thinking. These ideation sessions include people from different backgrounds (developers, project managers, content writers, etc.). I use creative exercises (such as Crazy Eights) to get as many ideas as possible.

I then wireframe different solutions using the inspiration found during ideation sessions and create prototypes for usability testing. Getting feedback from real users helps me narrow down the range of possible ideas and focus on the best ones.

After the first round of testing I make updates to the prototypes to fix obvious issues. One to two more rounds of tests with prototype updates in between results in a solution that is solid.

Evaluate & Iterate

When the MVP is released, I believe in setting key performance indicators (KPI) to help define success. I look at analytics to see where improvements can be made. I advocate for UX improvements to be prioritized to enhance the experience. I also look for customer feedback for ideas and features.
