Background

ALEKS is an artificial intelligence math and chemistry learning platform. Due to updates in common core math standards, ALEKS was tasked with introducing new math problems into its college algebra and geometry curriculum. Such problems required students to move, rotate, translate, and reflect objects over X and Y axes.

Prior to my arrival, UX designers had sketched concepts but ultimately relied on developers to create the tool in order to test it with real users. Due to the high investment of developing the tools, often times problems uncovered in usability testing were not implemented.

I was able to use advanced Axure techniques to prototype tools that closely mimicked how a real tool would behave. This allowed us to conduct usability testing without any actual development work.

UX Research

The research consisted of running usability-tests with real students in-house using interactive prototypes. I wrote the test plans and moderated usability tests. Our goal was to assess whether students could successfully use the tool. We had to write our test plans such that we separated learning from operating the input tool. To do that, I drew a graph on paper and had students use the tool to mimick my graph.      

UX Design

I used axure to create the tool interactions. These interactions were highly complex, but saved us tons of time and money by avoiding any actual development. We were able to uncover a few problems and redesign the tools before they went to development.
