Product Design

Audiomesh

Mobile Design, iOS Development

Background

Audiomesh is an app that allows musicians to collaborate more easily with one-another. The app allows a musician to record ideas with their phone, and instantly sync those audio files to the cloud and with band members, much like Dropbox or Google Drive.

UX Research

This project was an entrepreneurial effort. I used my own frustrations and experiences as a musician to inform the core idea. I also sent out a survey to bands on Craigslist and Facebook. These bands ranged from garage bands, to famous modern rock bands. Questions on the survey included:

What percentage of your time is spent writing original music? Have you ever recorded yourself playing music? What physical equipment do you use to record yourself playing music? What software do you use to record yourself playing music?

Once I had wire frames, I used the POP (Prototyping on Paper) app to guerilla test a few screens with musician friends.

I also conducted a competitive analysis with existing solutions. Most of these solutions were geared towards public collaboration with strangers. I assessed these platforms as well as messaged some of their users to understand mental models around collaboration.

UX Design

I did numerous iterations on the original concept in Adobe Illustrator. Because I was also coding the app, I also iterated on the concept in Xcode's storyboards.

I had tons of ideas for Audiomesh, but I reduced the app down to a couple core concepts: record audio, share audio privately, sync audio to the cloud. These are the primary use cases that require musicians to use multiple solutions. For example: Voice Memos for recording, email for sharing, and Dropbox for syncing.

The app is not finished. I am currently coding it in Swift. This will likely be a never-ending hobby project.
